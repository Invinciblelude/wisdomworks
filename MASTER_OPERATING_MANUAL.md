## 3. The "Loophole" Protection Toolkit

### The Stop Work Shield (Civil Code ¬ß 8830)

Use this if an owner stops paying. It kills the "Abandonment" charge.

**Step 1: Post the 5-Day Notice of Intent**
```
NOTICE OF INTENT TO SERVE STOP WORK NOTICE

TO: [Owner Name]
PROJECT: [Project Address]

NOTICE IS HEREBY GIVEN that Contractor intends to serve a Stop Work Notice 
pursuant to California Civil Code ¬ß 8830 if payment in the amount of $[Amount] 
is not received within FIVE (5) days from the date of posting this notice.

Date Posted: [Date]
Contractor: [Name]
License #: [License Number]
```

**Step 2: Serve the 10-Day Stop Work Notice**

If payment not received, serve via **Certified Mail, Return Receipt Requested**.

### The Lien Killer (Notice of Completion)

Recording this within 15 days of finishing "kills" the danger window.

- Shrinks GC lien rights to **60 days** (from 90)
- Shrinks Subcontractor lien rights to **30 days** (from 90)

### The "Paper GC" Contract (B&P ¬ß 7159)

Ensure every contract includes:

1. **$1,000/10% Down Payment Cap** (12-point bold type)
2. **Progress Payment Clause** (required statutory text)
3. **3-Day Right to Cancel** (5 days for seniors 65+, 7 days for disaster repair)
4. **Mechanics Lien Warning** (exact CSLB statutory language)
5. **Full & Unconditional Lien Release Clause**
6. **Change Order Clause** (written and signed)
7. **CGL Disclosure Statement**
8. **Workers' Comp Disclosure** (mandatory for all by 2028)
9. **Start & Completion Dates** (approximate)
10. **Signed Copy Disclaimer** (12-point bold type)

### Material Delivery Payment Clause (B&P ¬ß 7159.5)

**The Strategy**: Bill for materials upon delivery, not installation.

```
SECTION: PAYMENT FOR MATERIALS DELIVERED

Pursuant to California Business and Professions Code ¬ß 7159.5, the Contractor 
may request and accept payment for materials and equipment once they have been 
delivered to the project site and are under the control of the Owner, even if 
such materials have not yet been incorporated into the work.

Upon delivery of significant materials (e.g., lumber, windows, cabinetry, or 
appliances), Contractor shall provide an invoice for the value of said materials. 
Owner agrees to pay such invoice within three (3) days of delivery.

Ownership and title to the materials shall pass to the Owner upon payment, and 
Contractor shall provide a corresponding "Unconditional Waiver and Release on 
Progress Payment" for the value of the materials received.
```

### Three Advanced "Pro-Tips"

1. **The "Proof of Delivery" Photo**: Take a photo the second the delivery truck leaves. Attach to invoice.

2. **The "Insurance" Pivot**: Tell homeowner: "Once paid for, you own them. Notify your insurance for coverage while in your garage."

3. **The "Joint Control" Trigger**: For large jobs ($50k+), use Joint Control Service. Homeowner puts money in escrow; escrow pays supplier and contractor directly.

## 6. HIS (Home Improvement Salesperson) Program

### What is HIS?

A **Home Improvement Salesperson** is an individual registered under a licensed contractor who is authorized to solicit, sell, and negotiate home improvement contracts.

### Year 1 "Earn While You Learn" Model

1. **Day 1**: Submit HIS application to CSLB
2. **Week 1-4**: While HIS card processes, student does handyman work (‚â§$1,000)
3. **Month 2+**: HIS card approved, student begins selling contracts
4. **Commission**: Student earns % of gross profit on contracts they close

### Commission Structure

**Commission Rate**: 5-10% of Gross Profit
- Gross Profit = Total Contract Price - (Labor + Materials + Permits + Disposal)

**Payment Timing**:
- 50% upon successful clearing of initial milestone payment (after material delivery)
- 50% upon "Substantial Completion" and receipt of final payment from owner

### Legal & Ethics Compliance

**Critical Rules**:
1. **No Direct Payments**: Student shall NEVER accept a check or cash made out to their own name. All payments must be made to the School/Master entity.

2. **Full Disclosure**: Student must provide every homeowner with:
   - Notice of Right to Cancel (3-day, 5-day for seniors, 7-day for disaster)
   - All 2025/2026 mandated disclosures
   - CSLB-required contract clauses

3. **Conduct**: Any "high-pressure" sales tactics or misrepresentation of materials will result in immediate termination from the HIS registration and the Academy.

## 7. 2025/2026 Legal Updates

### SB 1455: Workers' Comp Delay & CSLB Email Requirement

**Effective**: January 1, 2025

#### Workers' Comp Delay
- **Original Date**: January 1, 2026
- **New Date**: January 1, 2028
- **Description**: Universal Workers' Comp mandate delayed
- **Exemption Trades**: C-39 Roofing, C-8 Concrete, C-20 HVAC, C-22 Asbestos, D-49 Tree Service
- **Savings**: Solo operators can avoid ~$2,000+ annual "ghost policy" until 2028

#### CSLB Email Requirement
- All licensees must provide monitored email address
- Must update CSLB within **30 days** of any change
- CSLB sends legal notices via email - "didn't see it" is NOT a defense

### AB 2622: Handyman Exemption Increase

**Effective**: January 1, 2025

- **New Limit**: $1,000 (up from $500)
- **Includes**: Labor + Materials + Tax
- **Permit Rule**: If project requires building permit, exemption does NOT apply

**Examples**:
- ‚úÖ Legal: $600 labor + $350 materials = $950
- ‚ùå Illegal: $600 labor + $450 window = $1,050
- ‚ùå Illegal: Water heater replacement (requires permit regardless of price)
- ‚ùå Illegal: New electrical circuit (requires permit)

**Required Disclaimer**: Must state on flyers: *"I am not a licensed contractor"*

### SB 61: Retention Cap Reduction

**Effective**: January 1, 2026

- **Old Cap**: 10%
- **New Cap**: 5%
- **Applies To**: Retention on private works contracts

### AB 1327: Notice of Right to Cancel Modernization

**Effective**: January 1, 2025

**Updates**:
- Email cancellation now accepted
- Must include contractor support phone number
- Must include contractor email address

### Financial "Loophole Protection Knowledge"

#### 1. "Special Order" Protection (Direct-to-Vendor Payment)
For high-cost custom materials, have the homeowner pay the supplier directly to bypass the $1,000 down payment cap.

#### 2. "Joint Control" Exception (B&P Code ¬ß 7159.5(a)(8))
For large projects, use a CSLB-approved third-party escrow service. This removes down payment and progress payment restrictions.

#### 3. "2% Monthly Penalty" (Civil Code ¬ß 8800)
If an owner wrongfully withholds payment, they owe 2% monthly interest. Include this in contracts.

#### 4. "Right to Stop Work" (Civil Code ¬ß 8830‚Äì8848)

#### 5. Equipment "Mobilization" Milestone
Include a contract milestone for "Site Preparation & Equipment Mobilization" to get paid when equipment arrives.

## 10. The Wisdom Works Honor Code

### The Moral Foundation

The Honor Code is the "moral glue" that ensures your savvy students don't turn into the "shady contractors" the CSLB is constantly trying to catch. By signing this on **Day 1**, they commit to a standard of professionalism that protects the school's reputation and their own future license.

### The Honor Code

**"Integrity in the Build. Honor in the Contract."**

As a student of Wisdom Works Academy, I understand that a California Contractor's License is a privilege, not a right. I pledge to uphold the following standards of conduct:

#### 1. Professionalism over Profit

**The $1,000 Rule**: I will never knowingly accept a down payment exceeding 10% or $1,000, whichever is less. I recognize that "cash flow" never justifies a misdemeanor.

**Transparent Change Orders**: I will never perform extra work without a signed, written Change Order. I will not use "surprises" to inflate a project's cost.

#### 2. Respect for the Craft & Code

**No Cut Corners**: I will build to the California Building Code (CBC) or higher. I understand that a "hidden" mistake is a future failure.

**Permit Integrity**: I will never suggest a homeowner "skip the permit" to save time or money. I protect the property value and safety of my clients.

#### 3. The Alumni Shield

**Brotherhood/Sisterhood of Builders**: I will not undercut a fellow Wisdom Works graduate on a bid through deceptive pricing. We compete on quality, not by "racing to the bottom."

**No Poisoning the Well**: I will never speak ill of a competitor's work to a client. I will let my own craftsmanship and documentation speak for itself.

#### 4. Absolute Financial Honesty

**Trust Funds**: I recognize that progress payments are held in trust. I will pay my material suppliers and subcontractors promptly. I will never use "Job B" money to pay for "Job A" materials.

**The HIS Pledge**: As a registered Home Improvement Salesperson, I will never represent myself as a licensed contractor until the CSLB officially grants me my license number.

#### 5. Safety as a Duty

**The 6-Foot Stand**: I will not work at heights above 6 feet without proper fall protection, nor will I allow anyone under my supervision to do so. A life is worth more than a deadline.
